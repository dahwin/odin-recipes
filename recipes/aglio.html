Aglio E Olio

A quick and light pasta made with Garlic (aglio) and Oil (olio).

Ingredients

  • 1-2 Servings of your choice of long pasta (Spaghetti or Linguini preferred)
  • 5-7 Garlic Cloves (depending on size of individual cloves)
  • 1-1 1/2 Tablespoon Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • 1 Tablespoon of Salt
  • 5-7 Sprigs of Fresh Parsley
  • (Optional) Dried Red Pepper Flakes (Gochugaru preferred)
  • (Optional) Parmigiano Reggiano Cheese (Whole Block preferred)

Steps

  1. Boil half a pot of water.
  2. Slice all garlic cloves as thin as possible. Some variation in slices is ok.
  3. Seperate parsley leaves from the sprigs. Chop finely.
  4. Pour the extra virgin olive oil on to a cold pan.
  5. Start heating pan slowly on low heat.
  6. If the water is at a rolling boil at this point, pour salt into water and wait an additional 1 minute.
  7. Boil pasta to al dente (normally 3-4 minutes less than what is listed on the packaging).
  8. Put garlic slices onto the pan, turn heat up to medium-low.
  9. Lightly cook garlic until garlic is taking on some color. If the garlic starts to cook too rapidy/fast, pour in 1/3 cup of pasta water to stop the cooking process.
  10. (Optional) Add in your red pepper flakes at this point and mix around with the garlic.
  11. Once pasta is al dente, trasnfer pasta into garlic oil sauce mixture. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water.
  12. Start combining pasta with garlic oil sauce, add in some pasta water gradually as you see the oil not clinging to the pasta. Chances are you'll most likely use only 1/3 of the reserved pasta water.
  13. Once sauce is combined with pasta, turn off heat, add your chopped parsley and give it a mix.
  14. Plate your pasta, and optionally at this point, you can top it off with some grated Parmigiano Reggiano cheese.
  15. Enjoy!
